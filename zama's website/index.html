Australia

Toolache wallaby

The toolache wallaby or Grey's wallaby is an extinct species of wallaby from southeastern South Australia and southwestern Victoria.

Bandicoot

The northern pig-footed bandicoot was a small species of extinct herbivorous Australian marsupial in the genus Chaeropus, the pig-footed bandicoots.

White-footed rabbit rat

The white-footed rabbit rat is an extinct species of rodent, which was originally found in woodlands from Adelaide to Sydney, but became restricted to south-eastern Australia.

Lesser bilby

The lesser bilby, also known as the yallara, the lesser rabbit-eared bandicoot or the white-tailed rabbit-eared bandicoot, is an extinct rabbit-like marsupial.

Frequently Asked Questions About Extinct Animals

What does it mean for an animal to be extinct?

An animal is considered extinct when there are no known surviving individuals of that species in the wild or in captivity.

What causes animal extinction?

Common causes of extinction include habitat destruction, climate change, pollution, overhunting, and invasive species.

Extinct Animals: Your Questions Answered

Extinct animals were once part of Earth's biodiversity but have disappeared due to various reasons.

How can we prevent animal extinctions?

Prevention involves habitat conservation, addressing climate change, enforcing wildlife protection laws, and reducing human impact on ecosystems.

What are some examples of extinct animals?

Notable examples include the dodo, woolly mammoth, saber-toothed cat, and passenger pigeon.
